Reliquum is an anomaly detection engine for Beyond Standard Model physics — hunting dark matter interactions, magnetic monopole signatures, and hierarchy-problem probes buried inside petabytes of detector and collider data.

The problem

Physics pipelines only find
what you already expect

Current experimental analysis relies on hard-coded, cut-based filters — designed by physicists who must pre-specify what they are looking for. Novel phenomena, by definition, cannot be found this way. Reliquum operates without prior signal assumptions.

01 — Dark Matter

Rare nuclear recoils submerged in background

WIMP signals — if they exist — amount to a handful of events per year inside tonnes of liquid xenon. Statistical separation from electron-recoil backgrounds requires a fundamentally different approach than signal-box counting.

02 — Monopoles

Topologically distinct tracks, no dedicated classifier

Magnetic monopoles leave slow, highly-ionising signatures unlike any Standard Model particle. No production-grade neural classifier exists that generalises across energy ranges without assuming a specific monopole mass.

03 — Hierarchy Problem

BSM resonances hidden in dijet and MET spectra

SUSY particles, composite Higgs states, and Kaluza-Klein excitations each leave distinct but subtle LHC signatures. Model-dependent searches miss the signatures of models not yet proposed.

Method

Simulation-based inference
meets ensemble anomaly scoring

Reliquum trains exclusively on Standard Model Monte Carlo and real background data. Any event statistically inconsistent with that learned distribution is flagged — without prior knowledge of what the new physics looks like.

01
Ingestion

Multi-source data ingestion

ROOT and HDF5 event files from XENONnT, LZ, IceCube, MoEDAL, and CERN Open Data are parsed via uproot and normalised into a unified 18-dimensional event tensor schema.

02
Simulation

GEANT4 background modelling

A GEANT4/NEST simulation engine generates high-fidelity Standard Model background events. These form the sole training distribution for all downstream models — no signal templates required.

03
Modelling

Three-branch ensemble

A Variational Autoencoder scores by reconstruction loss. A Normalising Flow (RQS architecture) scores by negative log-likelihood. A Graph Neural Network encodes detector hit topology for track-based signals. All three outputs feed a trained meta-classifier.

04
Inference

Posterior estimation via TMNRE

For confirmed anomalies, swyft (Truncated Marginal Neural Ratio Estimation) produces a posterior distribution over BSM parameters — WIMP mass, cross-section, monopole magnetic charge — ready for publication.

05
Explainability

SHAP feature attribution

Every flagged event receives a SHAP decomposition across all input features, identifying which physical observables drive the anomaly score — bridging the gap between neural classifiers and physicist intuition.
